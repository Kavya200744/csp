Fever

Fever is a common medical sign characterized by an elevated body temperature, usually above the normal range of 36.5–37.5°C (97.7–99.5°F). It is often a symptom of an underlying condition, typically an infection. Here's a detailed look at fever symptoms and common medications used to treat it.

Symptoms of Fever

  • Elevated Body Temperature: 38°C (100.4°F) is generally considered a fever.
  • Chills and Shivering:Feeling cold as the body temperature rises.
  • Sweating:As the fever breaks, the body may produce more sweat.
  • Headache: Often accompanying the fever.
  • Muscle Aches: Body aches and joint pain.
  • Weakness and Fatigue: Feeling tired or exhausted.
  • Dehydration: Increased fluid loss through sweat.
  • Loss of Appetite: Decreased desire to eat.
  • Irritability: Especially common in children.
  • Rapid Heartbeat and Breathing: The body works harder to maintain homeostasis.

Common Medications for Fever

Acetaminophen (Paracetamol):

Brand Names: Tylenol, Panadol
Dosage: Usually 500-1000 mg every 4-6 hours for adults. Follow pediatric dosage for children.
Mechanism: Reduces fever by acting on the hypothalamic heat-regulating center.

Ibuprofen:

Brand Names:Advil, Motrin
Dosage: Typically 200-400 mg every 4-6 hours for adults. Pediatric doses vary by weight.
Mechanism: Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) that reduces fever, pain, and inflammation.

Aspirin:

Brand Names: Bayer, Bufferin
Dosage: 325-650 mg every 4-6 hours for adults. Not recommended for children due to the risk of Reye's syndrome.
Mechanism: NSAID that works by reducing substances in the body that cause pain, fever, and inflammation.

Cold and Cough

Cold and cough are common illnesses that usually affect the upper respiratory tract. Here are the typical symptoms and some commonly recommended medicines:

Symptoms

Cold:

  • Runny or stuffy nose
  • Sore throat
  • Cough
  • Congestion
  • Slight body aches or a mild headache
  • Sneezing
  • Low-grade fever
  • General malaise

Cough:

  • Dry cough (non-productive)
  • Wet cough (productive with mucus)
  • Sore throat or hoarseness
  • Shortness of breath (in some cases)
  • Wheezing (in some cases)

Medicines

For Cold:

  1. Decongestants:
    • Pseudoephedrine (Sudafed)
    • Phenylephrine (Neo-Synephrine)
  2. Antihistamines:
    • Diphenhydramine (Benadryl)
    • Cetirizine (Zyrtec)
    • Loratadine (Claritin)
  3. Pain Relievers:
    • Dextromethorphan (Robitussin, Delsym)
  4. Cough Suppressants:
    • Guaifenesin (Mucinex)

For Cough:

  1. Dry Cough:
    • Cough suppressants like dextromethorphan
    • Lozenges and throat sprays
  2. Wet Cough:
    • Expectorants like guaifenesin to help thin and loosen mucus
    • Stay hydrated to help thin mucus

Home Remedies:

  • Hydration: Drink plenty of fluids, such as water, herbal teas, and broths.
  • Rest: Ensure adequate sleep and rest.
  • Humidifier: Use a humidifier to keep the air moist.
  • Saltwater Gargle: Gargle with warm salt water to soothe a sore throat.
  • Honey and Lemon: Mix honey and lemon in warm water to soothe the throat.
  • Steam Inhalation: Inhale steam from hot water to relieve congestion.
